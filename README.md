# model-fit-estimates repository

2026-02-04

This repository provides the different models used in the framework of WHO's Foodborne Disease Burden Epidemiology Reference Group (FERG) towards the global burden of foodborne disease. The repository contains models used to estimate the incidence/prevalence/case-fatality rate/mortality for different hazards.

The full methodology is outlined in *Methodological framework for the World Health Organization estimates of the global, regional and national burden of foodborne diseases 2026 edition*, submitted to xx. A preprint is available (https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.05.13.26353030). 
All FERG estimates have been published in Lancet global health:
* *WHO estimates of the global, regional, and national burden of 42 foodborne infectious and chemical hazards, 2000–21: an updated data synthesis*: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.langlo.2026.103994.
* *WHO estimates of the global, regional, and national burden of eight foodborne non-diarrhoeal enteric disease hazards, 2000 21: an updated data synthesis*: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.langlo.2026.103981.
* *WHO estimates of the global, regional, and national burden of 14 foodborne bacterial, protozoal, and viral diarrhoeal enteric diseases, 2000–21: an updated data synthesis*: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.langlo.2026.103997. 
* *WHO estimates of the global, regional, and national foodborne burdens of 14 invasive parasitic diseases, 2000–21: an updated data synthesis*: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.langlo.2026.103982.
* *WHO estimates of the global, regional and national disease burden of nine foodborne chemicals, 2000–21: an updated data synthesis*: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.langlo.2026.103986.

The source attribution methodology is described in *World Health Organization attribution of burden of foodborne diseases to transmission pathways and specific foods*. A preprint is available (https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-9449162/v1).
